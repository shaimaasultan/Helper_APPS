🧪 Full Transformation Pipeline Summary
Goal: Convert recognizable animal portraits into randomized, noise-like abstractions while retaining subtle emotional cues (especially in the eyes).

🔹 1. Puller Layer Transformation & Simulated Haze
- Purpose: Begin softening the image with a veil of mist.
- Method: Apply a semi-transparent layer with variable opacity and texture (e.g. cloud-like gradients).
- Effect: Blurs boundaries and introduces uneven pixel shifts.


🔹 2. Gaussian Blur (Soft Focus)
- Purpose: Mimic atmospheric haze and reduce edge sharpness.
- Method: Low-radius blur applied globally, with masking around key features.
- Effect: Smooths fur and background, erasing fine details.

🔹 3. Veil Overlay (White/Gray)
- Purpose: Simulate fog or mist.
- Method: Non-uniform veil with color tint (e.g. pale cyan or lavender), applied using blending modes like “soft light” or “screen.”
- Effect: Introduces unpredictable pixel interactions and color shifts.


🔹 4. Contrast & Saturation Reduction
- Purpose: Flatten dynamic range and mute colors.
- Method: Apply nonlinear tone curves and channel-specific saturation tweaks (e.g. reduce red by 40%, green by 20%).
- Effect: Creates asymmetric color loss and tonal warping.

🔹 5. Light Bloom Around Eyes
- Purpose: Preserve emotional connection.
- Method: Asymmetric, multi-layered radial glow centered on pupils.
- Effect: Eyes remain focal points even in heavy abstraction.


🔹 6. Facial Landmark Masking
- Purpose: Retain species-specific recognizability.
- Method: Aggressive masking of eyes, nose, ears to protect them from blur and veil.
- Effect: Ensures faint silhouette remains readable.

🔹 7. Atmospheric Noise / Grain
- Purpose: Simulate fog particles and add texture.
- Method: Overlay fine noise with spatial variation.
- Effect: Adds realism and breaks uniformity.


🔹 8. High-Frequency Digital Noise Injection
- Purpose: Push toward visual entropy.
- Method: Inject randomized RGB pixel noise across the frame.
- Effect: Makes the image resemble static or interference.

🔹 9. Nonlinear Color Shifts
- Purpose: Disrupt color harmony.
- Method: Apply hue warping, channel inversion, and tone remapping.
- Effect: Creates chaotic color scatter in the diff image.


🔹 10. Localized Bloom & Masking
- Purpose: Introduce asymmetry.
- Method: Vary bloom intensity across regions, mask selectively.
- Effect: Uneven pixel shifts and unpredictable glow zones.

🔹 11. Micro-Distortion / Warping
- Purpose: Break spatial alignment.
- Method: Apply subtle geometric distortion (e.g. wave, ripple, stretch).
- Effect: Causes pixel misalignment in diff maps.

🔹 12. Multi-Pass Masking & Blending
- Purpose: Layer complexity.
- Method: Blend multiple transformation passes with varied masks.
- Effect: Deepens abstraction and increases entropy.
