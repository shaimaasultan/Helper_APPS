Based on the investigation with 10 randomly generated 25x25 puzzles at 50% density, here's what I found regarding solve time variance:

Key Findings
Solve Times: Highly variable, ranging from 0.39s (fastest) to 197.74s (slowest), with an average of ~36s. Some puzzles failed to solve within 5 attempts (restarts), likely due to invalid configurations or excessive backtracking.
Total Clues: 296-331 (close to 50% of 625 = 312.5), but not the primary factor—slight variations don't correlate strongly with speed.
Box Clue Distribution: This is the main differentiator.
Min clues per box: 6-10 (out of 25 cells per box).
Max clues per box: 16-19.
Avg clues per box: 11.8-13.2.
Fast Puzzles: Tend to have higher min clues per box (e.g., 10) and slightly higher total clues (e.g., 331). Even distribution prevents "weak" boxes with too much freedom.
Slow/No-Solution Puzzles: Often have low min clues per box (e.g., 6), meaning some 5x5 boxes have only 6 clues (19 empty). This leads to exponential backtracking as the solver struggles with under-constrained regions.
Why the Variance?
Clue Distribution per Box: The most significant factor. Boxes with few clues allow more candidate combinations, increasing backtracking depth. Even if total density is 50%, uneven box filling (e.g., one box with 6 clues, another with 18) creates bottlenecks. The solver's MRV/degree heuristics prioritize constrained cells, but weak boxes delay progress.

Initial Propagation: Puzzles with more even clues allow better early pruning via naked/hidden subsets and forced moves. Uneven puzzles leave more cells ambiguous, requiring deeper search.

Random Transformations: The generator applies digit permutations, row/col swaps, and hole-poking. Some transformations create "hard" puzzles with hidden conflicts or poor constraint propagation, even at the same density.

Solved Cells Order: Not directly the issue—the solver dynamically chooses cells via heuristics. However, puzzles that propagate more initially (due to even clues) reach solvable states faster.

Other Factors: Restart thresholds (10k nodes) and decay help, but hard puzzles hit limits. No strong correlation with empty percentage per box beyond the min clues.

Recommendations
For Faster Solving: Ensure even clue distribution (e.g., modify _generate_dynamic_puzzle to enforce min clues per box >= 10). This could reduce variance.
Generator Improvement: Add balancing logic to avoid extreme box disparities.
Solver Tuning: Increase restart threshold or add more advanced propagation (e.g., implement naked triples) to handle uneven puzzles better.
The data shows uneven box clues are the root cause—puzzles with "weak" boxes (low min clues) are consistently slower or unsolvable. If you want to test with forced even distribution or more puzzles, let me know!
